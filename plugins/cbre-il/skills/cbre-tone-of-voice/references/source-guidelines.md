## Page 17
Writing Principles
Have a point of view
We have the courage to step up and share 
what we think. We state our opinions clearly 
and boldly, avoiding equivocation and vague 
statements.
Say it simply
Choose simple words over jargon and business-
speak and eliminate unnecessary adjectives. 
Keep in mind that we have a global audience, 
some of whom are reading in a second language. 
Our priority is to communicate clearly.
Remember that less is more 
We know that if we try to say too much, we end 
up overwhelming the reader with detail. That’s 
why we consider every word and focus on the 
most important things to communicate.
Convey action 
Use active—not passive—voice. Select verbs 
that show CBRE taking an active role, not just 
being a witness to something that is happening. 
(For example, “We created a solution” not “A 
solution was developed.”)
Don’t be punny 
While it might be tempting to write a headline 
that contains a clever pun, this is a trope of the 
commercial real estate industry and doesn’t set 
us apart from competitors. (For example, “Belly 
up to this premium bar space” or “Weaving a 
prime investment in a former cotton factory.”)
Be conversational, 
but professional
Too much formality prevents connection, so 
speak like a real person having a conversation. 
Relax the rules of grammar a little to allow 
for natural communication. It’s fine to use 
conjunctions. A sentence fragment can make a 
great headline. 
At the same time, avoid an overly friendly tone 
that gives away our credibility and expertise. (For 
example, “Let’s talk” works, while “Hey, what’s 
up??” is too informal.)
Don’t be egotistical 
Remember to balance bold statements about 
our capabilities with the benefits they provide 
to others so that we don’t sound arrogant. (For 
example, “We provide unmatched intelligence so 
that our clients can make the right decisions.”)
