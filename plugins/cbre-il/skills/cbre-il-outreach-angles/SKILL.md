# CBRE I&L Outreach Angles

This skill turns a target company into an **outreach sheet built as a single ranked list of about five to seven opportunities**, ordered by how developable each one is (the size and likelihood of an I&L transaction). It is written **plain-English-first**: the sheet opens with the situation in plain language, each opportunity is written as *what is happening, what it means for them, and your way in* before its sourced evidence, and a **jargon buster at the foot** defines every specialist term (so a broker who is not finance-trained can use it). Each opportunity also carries two labels, **developability** and **trigger strength** (event-driven if it has a dated forcing function, structural if it rests on a sourced company-specific inefficiency), a **readiness** label (send-now, or verify-first naming the one fact to confirm), a ready-to-send **email hook** and a **call opener**. It generates candidates across **all twelve angle families** (see `reference/source-playbook.md`) and an **all-European-country facility sweep**, scores each on both axes, and ranks on developability, so a big opportunity with a weak or structural trigger leads the list with its trigger labelled, instead of being buried for lacking a date.

**The deliverable is a self-contained, CBRE-branded HTML file** rendered from the gate-validated markdown by `helpers/render_html.py`: it leads with the plain-English situation and an at-a-glance table, and tucks each item's evidence behind a collapsible toggle so the default view is digestible and the rigour is one click away. The markdown is the checked source of truth; the HTML is what the broker reads and shares. Length is not capped: the sheet should be as long as it needs to be to explain the situation clearly.

It is a **prospecting and triage tool**. It tells you what to say, why it matters now, and which trigger makes the contact credible. It gives you the email hook to build on, but does **not** write the full outreach email, recommend a site or a strategy, or invent figures.

**Use this, not the account brief, when** the question is *why call them and what to lead with*: a fast, pre-contact triage. If the user wants the full picture of who the company is, how its supply chain works and where its property sits (a deck-length deliverable), that is `cbre-il-account-briefing`, not this skill.

## What it is and is not

The discipline that makes this useful, rather than just respectable, is that it points the research at the signals that actually sit on a senior operator's desk this quarter, not the ones every competing broker already read in the annual report. An opener built on public, lagging information proves only that you can read. An opener built on a leading indicator, a job posting in a market where the company has no facility, a CSCO appointed seven weeks ago, a planning application physically in motion, or a sourced structural tension (fast growth in a region still served from a distant hub) signals that you understand their world better than they have yet told anyone. That difference is the whole product.

Be honest about cost. The **output** is lightweight to read; the **research is not**: each run does real harvesting and will hit rate limits if fired at dozens of companies in one session. "Run it across many targets" means a sequence of fast runs, not one mega-run. Say so if the user expects to batch forty companies at once.

## The governing rule: evidence integrity and freshness

Nothing reaches the sheet that has not been checked against a real, retrievable, **dated** source. Every figure, date, facility count and named fact is reproduced as found, never estimated, rounded, recalled from training, or invented by the model. Any figure that cannot be sourced is omitted or marked as an assumption. Freshness is graded **live / ageing / background** and surfaces as a **readiness label** on each opportunity (send-now or verify-first), not as a gate that buries it; ranking is on developability, with trigger strength as a second label. Writing the sheet in plain English never means softening a fact: the jargon buster explains terms, it does not blur the numbers, which stay verbatim. This rule outranks every other instruction in this skill. When in doubt, leave the claim out.

**This skill requires live web retrieval.** The harvest and the reviewer's re-checks depend on real web search and fetch (the session's `WebSearch` / `WebFetch`, or an equivalent retrieval tool or MCP). If those tools are unavailable, the skill must STOP and say so plainly; authoring a sheet from training memory is never an acceptable fallback and violates the governing rule. Confirm availability at Stage 0.

The full rule, the freshness grades and how to compute them, the evidence file the orchestrator writes, the broker-facing Source Ledger, the re-retrieval protocol, the degraded-mode rule and the anti-hallucination standard are in **`reference/evidence-and-ledger.md`. Read it first.**

**Completeness is part of integrity, and it is enforced, not just asserted.** Under-counting is as damaging as inventing: telling a head of supply chain they run one warehouse when they run four proves the opposite of homework. The pipeline must check not only that each claim is true but that the set is complete; nothing else tries to prove the evidence file incomplete, so this discipline is explicit. Facts about the physical footprint (site count, locations, size) are **enumerated to saturation and triangulated across sources**, never taken from the first hit, and a single-source count is a draft rather than an answer. Footprint completeness is **coverage of every country in a fixed European set**, established by an all-Europe facility-evidence fan-out that is NEVER scoped to where the company sells, because a warehouse or manufacturing plant can sit in a country with no stores and no sales. It ships as a machine token (`RECONCILIATION: RECONCILED` only when every country in the set is evidence-positive with a source or evidence-absent after a real native-language search, otherwise `UNRESOLVED GAP`); a company-stated DC total is only a corroborating cross-check, never the control. An unresolved gap is the normal state for a large occupier and blocks only saturation or complete-network LANGUAGE on the sheet, never the shipping of opportunities, and is never back-filled with legacy or assumed sites. **Absence is a gap, never evidence:** "not found in my searches" can never become "it does not exist". An opportunity may rest on a non-existence only when a source positively states it (for example the company says supply is centralised in one site, which is also exactly what powers a structural trigger), and the reviewer kills any item whose thesis leans on an unsourced not-found. The output stays readable, but the research goes deep: the depth, footprint-completeness and reconciliation rules are in `reference/source-playbook.md`, and a site count is a load-bearing number governed by `reference/evidence-and-ledger.md`.

## Inputs (Stage 0)

**This skill chooses the angle.** Not knowing the hook is the whole reason to run it, so the skill never asks the user which angle, focus or direction to take. The only thing it needs is the company (and ideally the market); everything else it discovers and decides from the evidence. Focus and the door are optional context a user may volunteer, never questions the skill puts to them. Collect and validate the following, then run end to end without quizzing the user.

**Capability precondition (check before harvesting).** Confirm live web search and fetch are available with one trivial probe (a single query that should return a dated result). If retrieval is unavailable, blocked, or returns nothing retrievable, STOP and report "cannot run without live web access" rather than proceeding. Do not author from memory; a sheet with no real sources behind it is worse than no sheet.

**Run date (anchor first).** Record today's date as the run date and compute **all** recency relative to it (live vs ageing vs background, "appointed seven weeks ago", "first hundred days"). Recency claims are meaningless without this anchor. The run date goes in the sheet header.

**Required: the company.** The exact company name (the legal entity, so the harvest does not drift onto a namesake). If the user did not name a market, do not stop to ask: default to the company's primary operating geography plus a pan-European view, and say which lens you used in the header.

**Listed or private (routes the harvest).** Determine at scope lock whether the target is publicly listed or privately held. This changes which sources exist and how the harvest is weighted: a private family-owned distributor has no analyst Q&A, no capex guidance and no activist disclosure, so the pressure machine pivots to statutory filings and the leading-indicator agents carry more of the load. See the public/private branch in `reference/source-playbook.md`.

**Output language.** Default English (UK English). Do not ask. Write the authored text in another Latin-script European language only if the user volunteers one; sourced figures and quotes always stay verbatim in their source language. If a non-Latin-script language is requested, say it is not yet supported.

**Focus (optional, never solicited).** Do not ask the user for a focus or a direction; finding the strongest opportunity is the skill's job. Discover and choose from the evidence. If, and only if, the user volunteers a focus (automation, ESG, consolidation, cost reduction and so on), treat it as a ranking weight, never a filter.

**The door (optional, never solicited).** Do not ask. If the user volunteers the stakeholder they can already reach, use it: an opportunity whose natural stakeholder is reachable ranks higher within its developability band. If no door is given, rank on merit and name the natural stakeholder for each item; never block waiting for one.

**Known context (optional).** Any existing relationship, prior meeting, known requirement, or competitor incumbency. Treat this as ground truth the sheet must not contradict and should build on.

## Agentic design

The architecture is an orchestrator, three parallel context-isolated harvest agents, an author pass, and one isolated reviewer. The resonance of the output is decided in the **topology**, not in the writing: the agents are pointed at leading indicators first, so generic angles are starved of evidence before anyone writes a word.

- **Orchestrator (this thread).** Locks the scope and reads the known context, dispatches the three harvest agents, and **owns the evidence file: it is the only writer.** It merges the three agents' returned findings blocks into that single file (deduping and resolving conflicts per the source playbook), **builds and clears the chase list before authoring** (see below), runs the author pass off it, dispatches the reviewer, adjudicates the verdict, and assembles the sheet, then renders the HTML. It never self-certifies a judgement about work it authored.

- **Three harvest agents, parallel, each with its own context window.** Each timestamps every finding and **returns it as a findings block tagged with its agent prefix (A/B/C); it does not write the shared file.** A context-isolated subagent cannot append to shared state, so it hands its block back to the orchestrator, which is the only process that writes the evidence file. An agent that cannot find something returns an explicit gap, never a guess. Each agent's source classes and query templates are in `reference/source-playbook.md`.
  - **Agent A, the now machine.** Leading indicators and live triggers first: job postings, executive appointments, planning and permit filings, just-closed M&A, refinancing windows, contract-renewal clocks, opening and closure programmes in motion.
  - **Agent B, the pressure machine.** For a listed company: the latest results, **privileging the analyst Q&A over the prepared remarks**, capex direction, balance-sheet clocks, activist involvement, restructuring. For a private company: statutory filings, registered charges, PE-sponsor hold clocks and credit signals instead.
  - **Agent C, the footprint machine, is a two-phase all-Europe fan-out, not one agent.** Phase 1 dispatches one cheap (Haiku) agent per country in a fixed European set (NEVER scoped to where the company sells), each searching English AND the local language for evidence of any facility (DC, warehouse, fulfilment, cross-dock, or manufacturing/production plant) and returning a structured per-country verdict (`EVIDENCE-POSITIVE` / `EVIDENCE-ABSENT` / `INCONCLUSIVE`). Phase 2 runs Sonnet-tier deep enumeration ONLY in evidence-positive countries. Completeness is coverage of the whole set, an empty search is never a "no site" finding, and a facility in a non-sales country is surfaced as an opportunity, not a table. Full design in `reference/source-playbook.md`.

- **Chase list (orchestrator, between merge and author).** Any returned finding flagged "pull if possible", "filed or announced on a date, verify", or any pointer the agent located but did not open, becomes a **chase item**. The orchestrator records these in a `## Chase list` block in the evidence file and must **resolve every one before the author pass begins**: either fetch it and write the retrieved value into the evidence file as a normal dated finding, or, if it cannot be retrieved, close it as an explicit logged gap (`gap:` with the reason). **No unopened "pull if possible" pointer may survive into the author pass.** The full rule and the deterministic check are in `reference/evidence-and-ledger.md`.

- **Author pass.** Walks **every angle family**, runs each candidate through the four-link chain (which ends in an email hook and a call opener), then **scores each candidate on two independent axes**: developability and trigger strength (event-driven or fenced structural). It builds a **single ranked list of about five to seven opportunities, ordered by developability**, each carrying both axis labels, a readiness label, and, in plain English, *what is happening, what it means for them, and your way in* before the sourced evidence. It also writes the document's plain-English scaffolding: the **situation in plain English**, a **why this is a live prospect** note, an **at-a-glance table**, and a **jargon buster at the foot** defining every specialist term the sheet uses. A high-developability, weak-trigger opportunity ranks at the TOP with its trigger labelled, never demoted for lacking a date. Generic external trends and the unsound do not make the list; they go to the watch-list or are dropped.

- **Reviewer, isolated, no stake in the drafts.** Re-retrieves the **load-bearing** numbers in each item within a fixed fetch budget, then validates each item on **both axes**: that the **trigger** is either a real dated event or a **properly fenced structural inefficiency** (two sourced company-specific facts, not a generic trend), that the **developability** rating is honest, and that the **readiness** label is right so no inference ships as a send-now fact. It also checks that **every specialist term is defined in the jargon buster** and that the plain-English fields are actually plain, not restated jargon. It kills items resting on a generic undated trend or an unsourced not-found, and returns a clean "do not call" with a watch-list when nothing is developable enough. It returns verdicts; the orchestrator fixes and re-runs with a fresh reviewer. **Loops are bounded:** at most one fix-and-recheck cycle per item. The full checklist is in `reference/evidence-and-ledger.md`.

**No-subagent fallback.** Where sub-agents are unavailable, run the three harvests in sequence in one context, appending each finding to the evidence file as you go, then **flush that working context and re-read only the evidence file** before the reviewer pass, so author-reviewer separation survives.

## The pipeline

- **Stage 0. Scope lock.** Confirm web retrieval is available. Anchor the run date. Validate inputs, read the known context, fix the geography lens and language, and determine listed vs private.
- **Stage 1. Harvest and merge.** Dispatch Agents A and B for triggers and Agent C as the **all-Europe facility-evidence fan-out**. Search every country in its **own language as well as English**. Timestamp every finding. Each returns a prefixed findings block (or an explicit gap); the orchestrator merges the three into the single evidence file in one write. Then **build the chase list and clear it** before any authoring.
- **Stage 2. Extract and tag.** Pull candidate signals. Tag each as fact, inference or assumption, and grade recency as live, ageing or background against the run date. Before finalising any **ageing** grade on a trigger, run the **recency-refresh micro-pass**: one query for a newer confirmation, recording the outcome token.
- **Stage 3. Translate.** Walk **every angle family** and, for each, either run the four-link chain on a candidate anchored to a dated fact or a sourced structural tension, or log an explicit `no-signal` line. Generate a broad field of candidates, never fabricating one to fill a family.
- **Stage 4. Score on two axes, rank, label.** Score every survivor on developability and trigger strength. **Rank by developability, highest first**, with trigger strength a visible label, not a gate. Give each item a readiness label. A real, sourced opportunity is never demoted for a weak or structural trigger; only a generic undated trend goes to the watch-list, and only the unsound is killed.
- **Stage 5. Review.** The isolated reviewer re-retrieves the load-bearing numbers, confirms dates, checks the fact-versus-inference labelling and the two-axis and jargon-buster discipline, runs the **right-angle / attribution check** (each trigger belongs to THIS entity and implies the claimed consequence, killing true-but-mis-attributed items) and the **completeness falsification pass** against the European coverage map, kills any item resting on an unsourced not-found, and can downgrade the sheet to "do not call". Re-runs are bounded to one fix-and-recheck cycle per item. See `reference/evidence-and-ledger.md`.
- **Stage 6. Assemble, gate, render.** Build the markdown sheet and Source Ledger to `reference/output-template.md`. Run the deterministic gate from the skill directory: `python helpers/final_gate.py <sheet.md> --ledger <ledger.md> --evidence <evidence.md>` (resolve the helper against the skill's own folder; on Windows run it with native Python via the MCP shell). It must return `STATUS: PASS` before delivery; fix any FAIL and re-run. Then render the deliverable: `python helpers/render_html.py <sheet.md> --ledger <ledger.md>`, producing the self-contained, CBRE-branded HTML. Deliver the `.html` as the primary artefact, with the validated `.md` and the ledger alongside. Finish with the final QA self-check in the template.

## The now test, re-axed: developability first, trigger strength as a label

Prospecting needs two independent axes, and the old design collapsed them into one: it gated on whether a dated trigger existed, so a big opportunity with a weak date was buried while a thin but well-dated one floated to the top. The fix is to **rank on developability and treat trigger strength as a second, labelled axis, never a hard gate.**

A valid trigger is one of two classes, and **neither is auto-demoted**:
- **Event-driven:** a dated forcing function with a clock on it (a lease break or expiry; a refinancing window or bond maturity with a near date; a 3PL contract likely up for tender; a results event in the last few weeks; a planning decision; a new senior executive inside their decision window; a just-closed acquisition; a closure or opening programme in motion; a dated compliance deadline).
- **Structural:** a sourced, company-specific inefficiency or tension with no single clock, which for cold outreach is often the strongest hook there is. It must be **fenced**: it rests on two company-specific, separately sourced facts whose tension is the hook (for example rapid growth in one country set against distribution still run from a distant hub), both tagged [FACT], with the inefficiency itself tagged [INFERENCE]. A single sourced fact plus a generalisation is a trend, not a structural hook, and is killed.

What is still **not** a trigger and never reaches the ranked list: a generic external trend with no company-specific sourced anchor ("they are expanding", "sustainability is rising up the agenda"). These go to the watch-list with the dated event that would activate them.

The freshness distinction lives inside each item as a **readiness label**: send-now when the trigger is dated or live and the load-bearing facts re-retrieve, or verify-first naming the single fact to confirm before sending. Apply all of this at Stage 4.

## The four-link translation chain

Apply this chain to each candidate signal, across **every angle family** (see `reference/source-playbook.md`), and generate far more than you keep:

**Company signal -> business implication -> real estate or supply chain implication -> broker-relevant opportunity.**

Then, for each surviving opportunity, produce **the plain-English layer and two concrete outputs**: the plain-English *what is happening / what it means for them / your way in*; a cold-email hook (a first line referencing something specifically true about THEM, the one consequence that makes it matter now, and a soft low-friction ask); and a call opener (the peer-pattern question for a live conversation). The email hook is the deliverable the salesperson acts on; the call opener is for when they get someone on the phone.

Each surviving opportunity must answer, in substance and not as a visible checklist: why this company, why now (or why this chronic structural gap matters now), what the concrete issue is, why a senior stakeholder would care, what an I&L broker can credibly discuss, and what is plausibly on the relevant leader's mind. That last point is an inference and is labelled as one, tied to the specific evidence and the stakeholder's **actual remit**, not generic worry.

The opener's **peer pattern** is the differentiator and also the highest hallucination risk: it must be grounded in cited examples or framed explicitly as CBRE's observed experience, never asserted as an invented statistic. The grounding rule is in `reference/evidence-and-ledger.md`, and it applies equally to the email hook and the call opener.

## Ranking: developability first, trigger strength as the second axis

Rank the single list **by developability, highest first**: how big and transactable the I&L opportunity is. Trigger strength is the **second axis, shown as a label on every item (event-driven or structural), never a gate**: it tells the salesperson how defensible the hook is and breaks ties within a developability band, but it does not move a big opportunity down the list. Within a band, weigh evidence strength (primary beats trade-press beats rumour), trigger strength, commercial actionability, focus fit (a weight, not a filter), and stakeholder seniority and reachability.

**Output is one ranked list of about five to seven opportunities, not a thin pair and not a two-tier shortlist-plus-longlist.** The quality bar is kept by the developability threshold, the structural-trigger fence and the reviewer, not by a freshness gate: integrity comes from **labelling confidence and trigger strength honestly, not from suppressing the best opportunity**. Fear manufactured certainty, not a weak date on a real opportunity.

## Fact, inference, assumption convention

Keep these visibly separate throughout, because the broker needs to know which parts they can state and which they must phrase as a question. Tag inline with the brackets exactly as written, because the deterministic gate keys on them:
- **[FACT]** Retrieved from a dated source, reproduced as found.
- **[INFERENCE]** A reasoned read built on facts; the inference and the facts it rests on are both shown. A structural trigger's inefficiency is an [INFERENCE] resting on its two sourced [FACT]s.
- **[ASSUMPTION]** A working assumption where evidence is absent; flagged as such, never dressed as fact.

Any statement about what a stakeholder is thinking is an **[INFERENCE]** tied to named evidence and that person's remit.

## User preferences (always)
- Write in the chosen output language, default English, UK English when English. Sourced figures and quotes stay verbatim in their source language.
- **Plain English first.** Lead every opportunity and the whole sheet with plain language a broker who is not finance-trained can follow; define every specialist term in the jargon buster; assume the reader is smart but not an MBA. Explaining a term never means blurring the number behind it.
- No em or en dashes anywhere.
- Full sentences, never bullet fragments, in the output prose. Lead with the so-what; never a data dump.
- Be critical and honest: thin opportunities, stale evidence and incumbents are reported straight. Give a broad, honestly-labelled ranked list rather than a thin pair of fragile certainties; say "do not call" when nothing is developable enough, but still surface the watch-list and the dated event that would change that.
- Edit, do not rebuild: when re-running with updated inputs, redo only the affected stages, reassemble the sheet, and re-render the HTML.
